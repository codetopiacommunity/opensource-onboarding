# Day 05 — Review Someone Else's PR

Welcome back. 👋

Yesterday you opened your first Pull Request. Today you are on the other side — you are the reviewer.

Code review is one of the most important skills in open source and professional development. It is how teams catch mistakes, share knowledge, and maintain quality. Being a good reviewer is just as valuable as being a good contributor.

Today you will review a PR from someone else in the cohort.

---

## What You Will Do Today

- Understand what a good code review looks like
- Find an open PR to review
- Leave a meaningful review comment
- Approve or request changes

---

## Step 1 — What Makes a Good Review

Before you start, understand what you are looking for. A good review is not about finding fault. It is about making the work better.

When reviewing a profile PR, ask yourself:

- Is the file named correctly? It should be `their-github-username.mdx`
- Did they follow the template structure?
- Are there any obvious typos or formatting issues?
- Is the frontmatter filled in correctly? No empty fields left from the template

You are not expected to be an expert. You are expected to look carefully and give honest, respectful feedback.

> Hint: If everything looks good, say so clearly. "Looks good to me, nothing to change" is a valid and useful review.

---

## Step 2 — Find a PR to Review

Go to the main repo on GitHub:

```
https://github.com/codetopiacommunity/open-source-get-started
```

Click the **Pull Requests** tab at the top.

What you should see: a list of open PRs from your cohort members.

Pick one that has not been reviewed yet. You will see a label or no comments if it has not been touched.

> Hint: Do not pick your own PR. Do not pick the same PR someone else is already reviewing — check the comments first to see if someone has started.

---

## Step 3 — Read the Changes

Click on the PR you chose. You will see:

- The PR title and description at the top
- A **Files changed** tab showing exactly what was added or modified

Click **Files changed**.

What you should see: the new profile file with all the content highlighted in green. Green means added content.

Read through it carefully. Check everything from Step 1.

---

## Step 4 — Leave a Comment

There are two ways to leave a comment:

**On a specific line**
Hover over any line in the Files changed view. A blue `+` button will appear on the left. Click it to leave a comment on that specific line. Use this when you have feedback about something specific.

**On the whole PR**
Scroll down to the bottom of the PR page. You will see a comment box. Use this for general feedback.

Write your comment clearly and respectfully. For example:

If something needs fixing:
```
The file is named "john.mdx" but your GitHub username is "johndoe". 
Can you rename it to "johndoe.mdx"?
```

If everything looks good:
```
Looks good to me. Profile is complete and follows the template correctly.
```

> Hint: Never leave a one word comment like "good" or "fix this." Always explain what you mean. The person reading your review should know exactly what to do next.

---

## Step 5 — Submit Your Review

After leaving your comment, submit your review formally.

Click the **Review changes** button at the top right of the Files changed tab.

You will see three options:

- **Comment** — leave feedback without approving or blocking
- **Approve** — you are happy with the changes, they can be merged
- **Request changes** — something needs to be fixed before this can be merged

Choose the right one based on what you found. Write a short summary in the text box and click **Submit review**.

What you should see: your review is now visible on the PR for everyone to see.

> Hint: If you are unsure whether to approve or request changes, choose Comment. It is better to give feedback without a verdict than to approve something you are not sure about.

---

## Quick Reference — What You Learned Today

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| Code review | Examining someone else's changes and giving feedback |
| Files changed | The tab showing exactly what was added or modified in a PR |
| Approve | You confirm the changes are good to merge |
| Request changes | You flag issues that need to be fixed before merging |
